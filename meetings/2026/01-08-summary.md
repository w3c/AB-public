## 2026-01-08 Advisory Board/Board of Directors joint meeting

What follows is a brief summary of the topics discussed in the W3C Advisory Board/Board of Directors joint call on 2026-01-08.


attending: Amy van der Hiel, An Qi (Angel) Li,  Avneesh Singh, Brent Zundel, Chunming Hu, Chris Needham, Chris Wilson, Wei Ding, Daniel Appelquist , Dominique Hazaël-Massieux, Elena Lape , Emma Fraser, Eric Siow, Florian Rivoal, Max Gendler, Hidde de Vries, Theresa O'Connor, Tatsuya Igarashi, Koichi Moriyama, Hiroshi Ota, Song Xu, Léonie Watson


* **Consortium Member Representatives vs AC-Reps · Issue #247 Discussion**  

The AB and Board discussed the descriptions and distinctions between Member Associations, AC Representatives and Consortium Member Representatives in the W3C Process and Bylaws.

The topic was raised to address a proposal to simplify of the roles of Advisory Committee Reps and Consortium Members in the Process and Bylaws. It was noted that Member Associations are not Consortium Members, so do not have a Consortium Member representative, but do have an AC Representative. It was explained that the initial goal of defining Member Associations (eg: consortia, user societies or individuals and companies joining for common goals) was to help maintain the one org/one vote membership value. It was also mentioned that Member Associations could also be a means for people to otherwise avoid paying separate membership dues. The Process and Bylaws both define Member Associations, but those definitions differ, so it was suggested to fix those differences.

The differences in roles/rights between AC reps and Consortium Members was discussed including that Consortium Members vote for the Board, Bylaws, and significant changes to the organisation, e.g., mergers or dissolution, whereas AC reps vote on charters and specifications, and in AB and TAG elections.

It was suggested that this distinction causes confusion, and there's also no easy way to identify or contact all the Consortium Member reps, to discuss issues affecting the Consortium, e.g., during Board election campaigns. There has also been low attendance at Consortium Member Meetings.

The group discussed that there’s great value in keeping the role of the AC rep simple and that AC reps need to understand the Process while Consortium Member reps might need to have more managerial or legal understanding. It was suggested that driving the technical direction of standards vs governing a corporation might need different skills. Some people felt there was value in maintaining a distinction between the two roles and one person confirmed they’d gotten feedback from an AC rep that they’d like to keep the roles separate.

It was agreed there might be some confusion about the differences in the roles and that there’s good reason to simply the organization structure by combining the AC rep and Consortium Member rep roles Giving different Members flexibility to have different approaches was seen by some as valuable but there was a general sense in the discussion that the roles could be combined.

While it was suggested that the urgency on the question might not be high it was seen as a good idea to reach out to the Membership for their thoughts.
