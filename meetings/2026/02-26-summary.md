## 2026-02-26 Advisory Board meeting

What follows is a brief summary of the topics discussed in the W3C Advisory Board call on 2026-02-26

attending: Daniel Appelquist, Wei Ding, Seth Dobbs, Emma Fraser, Max Gendler, Tatsuya Igarashi, Elena Lape, Theresa O'Connor, Hiroshi Ota, Avneesh Singh, Hidde de Vries, Amy van der Hiel, Brent Zundel, Song Xu

* **Info Share: Updates from the Team and Community**
Team shared information on the update to the "no cross posting" policy. The AB asked for clarification about WGs posting to each other and Team confirmed they would confirm  Team encouraged the AB to put forward ideas for Breakouts Day on 25-26 March and shared the reminder of the time change starting in the US on 8 March (and in Europe and elsewhere 3 weeks later). Dan noted that the meeting time is tied to UTC so would change for the US but be the same for others for the next few weeks. 


* **Open Issue Review & Triage**
Brent lead the AB through a review of GH issues marked as "candidates to close". The AB discussed multiple issues, many of which had already been completed or resolved successfully through events and so actioned them to be closed. 


* **Feb Monthly Member Meeting read out**
The AB members who led sessions during the February Monthly Member meeting gave overviews of the discussions. Hidde, Brent and Igarashi noted that there was wide discussion about the ETSI browser security draft standard including: on whose behalf such a response might be made and  that it might be couched as from W3C rather than representing every member; that since the project relates to standards in the EU it is important; that wide feedback is appropriate; and that recognition of W3C as a source of expertise in the area is good. Dan offered to relay feedback from the AC and AC to Simone. 

The session leaders also mentioned discussions including: topics for AC 2026 and questions about timing for the DevMeetup; a potential collaboration with GS1 on humans and LLMs; normative references; and rechartering vs maintenance of the WoT Group. Session leaders also mentioned discussions of AC and Consortium Representatives and it was noted some members felt the roles were different and should not be combined. 

* **Time with Seth**
Seth noted he was happy to talk about anything the AB wanted. On the Consortium Member vs. AC Rep discussion from the previous agenda he noted that instead of a few people talking to a small number of members he felt that getting a more comprehensive understanding of what the Membership thinks would be better. He noted that many people didn’t know there was a distinction between the roles at all. He proposed that the Team craft a survey which includes information so Members can make an informed decision. He noted that since this is about corporate governance we want to move swiftly but appropriately. AB members noted that including the smaller number of Members who already have different people in the roles is valuable; they agreed that understanding the gravity of the matter is important and shouldn’t be discussed without context so supported developing an explainer. Igarashi noted that several members attending the third AB-led Member meeting session indicated that they would prefer to keep the two different roles. Seth agreed that distinctions between program and corporate governance is important and that the poll would not be a vote, but rather, information for a thoughtful dialogue by Members. Several AB members noted they were interested in helping. Seth agreed that he and Amy would work on an explainer and bring back to the AB the following week.
