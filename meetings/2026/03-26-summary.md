
## 2026-03-26 Advisory Board meeting

What follows is a brief summary of the topics discussed in the W3C Advisory Board meeting on 2026-03-26

attending: Daniel Appelquist, Wei Ding, Max Gendler, Tatsuya Igarashi, Yves Lafon, Elena Lape, An Qi (Angel) Li, Theresa O'Connor, Simone Onofri, Hiroshi Ota, Avneesh Singh, Amy van der Hiel, Brent Zundel

Full minutes (member-only link): https://www.w3.org/2026/03/26-ab-minutes.html

* **Info Share**
Amy gave  an update including the upcoming time change; that Team are preparing for the AC meeting; and some updates on Breakouts Day. Elena noted that the AB position statement on "Use of Large Language Models in Standards Work" has been published and there will be a blog post. This is the first AB position statement.  https://www.w3.org/TR/llms-standards/

* **Open Issue Review & Triage**
Brent led a review of open Issues in the AB repo. AB members discussed and closed issues including WCAG to ISO; election transparency; and AB minutes. The topic of appeals of disciplinary sanctions was slated for possible AB/BoD discussion. 

* **Planning for Apr F2F in Hangzhou**
Angel and Dan reviewed proposed agenda topics for the upcoming AB F2F. It was confirmed that the issue on AC Reps and Member Consortium Reps would be put forward to poll Members and those involved in discussions would be alerted. Other agenda topics included: a Human Rights and Standards position statement; Process refactoring; Incubation of new work; and time for feedback on the preceding AC meeting. 

* **W3C Council on the Formal Objection to Digital Credentials**
Simone and Yves joined the call for discussion on the status of an upcoming council on an FO on Digital Credentials. Yves noted that the Team Report should be done this week and once a chair is named, the council can start.  Simone gave some background. The AB noted that the question of this FO is less technical than about what kind of things can be normally referenced which is more in the scope of the AB; and there was encouragement for someone on the AB to be chair.
