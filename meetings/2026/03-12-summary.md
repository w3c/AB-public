## 2026-03-12 Advisory Board meeting

attending: Daniel Appelquist, Hidde de Vries, Wei Ding, Seth Dobbs, Emma Fraser, Max Gendler, Tatsuya Igarashi, Elena Lape, An Qi (Angel) Li, Theresa O'Connor, Hiroshi Ota,  Ralph Swick, Amy van der Hiel, Song Xu, Brent Zundel

Full minutes (member-only link): https://www.w3.org/2026/03/12-ab-minutes.html

* **Infoshare**

* **Team appointed TAG seats**
Ralph discussed possible modifications to the Process to clarify the language and adjust the tenor of the expectations around Team TAG appointments. He noted that Team solicits input from the TAG, Membership and community on who to appoint and specifically asks the TAG what skills they would like to add to the Group. He explained the Team-TAG selection committee identifies people to approach to ask if they would be interested and willing (not all are able to do so) and then when they have enough candidates, they ask the AB and TAG to ratify. He requested that the tenor of the ratification step should be weighted to supporting the efforts Team has put in and their conclusions and that ratification should be a point for TAG and AB to object only if members of the ratifying bodies identify something the Team may have not known or overlooked. AB members noted the current system does allow for simple up/down voting and serves as an "emergency break" for when needed. Some AB did question why they were involved in the ratification. The group discussed past situations and agreed that things were getting better.

The AB discussed preparations for the Tuesday 17 March Monthly Member meeting. Angel shared some potential topics and asked for AB feedback. It was agreed the topics were good and suggested to also ask the AC for topics on slack and to remind attendees about the DST time difference. Hidde, Tess and Angel agreed to chair different sessions 

* **Review the FPWD of AB Position Paper on Large Language Models in standards work**
Hidde and Elena talked about the AB position paper on LLMs and standards work and noted the intention to publish as a Note. They acknowledged that  AI is a contentious subject but that the paper deliberately took a balanced approach to try to give useful advice. They clarified that the Note is not an AC reviewed W3C Statement but an opinion of the AB. They asked the AB if there was consensus to publish. Some AB asked for more time to review but the group were resolved to publish next week. Hidde and Elana noted they would work with the Team on preparing for publication.
