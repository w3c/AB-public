## 2026-02-04 Advisory Board F2F Day 1 

What follows is a brief summary of the topics discussed in the W3C Advisory Board F2F hybrid meeting/call on 2026-02-04

Attending: Daniel Appelquist, François Daoust, Hidde de Vries, Wei Ding,  Seth Dobbs, Emma Fraser,  Max Gendler,  Tatsuya Igarashi, Elena Lape, An Qi (Angel) Li,  Chris Needham, Theresa O'Connor, Hiroshi Ota, Florian Rivoal, Avneesh Singh, Amy van der Hiel, Léonie Watson, Chris Wilson

Full minutes (member-only link): https://www.w3.org/2026/02/04-ab-minutes.html


* **W3C Chapters program**
Angel presented an update of the Chapters program, giving a history of its inception as W3C Offices (and the  funding that initially supported it); changes over time (including linking chapters with member recruitment);  when she took over in 2023; and the present state. Several AB noted they found Offices very helpful and said that there's real value in having local support/interest in different countries. Some felt this style of support could be linked to our W3C strategy. The initial idea of Chapters as a way to gather developers and the community was seen as important. The group discussed the potential of Offices/Chapters in engaging with the wider community and focusing on organizations who share alignment with us while also being cautious about who can speak in the W3C's name. The group acknowledged the need for member recruitment, whether via Chapters or not. They resolved that they see great potential in the Chapters program to benefit W3C and the global W3C community, and advise the team to integrate this program into its overall strategy.  


* **Plans for AC 2026**
François joined the meeting to discuss plans for AC 2026. He thanked the AB who have been participating on the Program Committee. He noted the goal of the AC agenda is to trigger discussions in the membership. There will be a mix of presentations and small group discussions with an agenda structured around the Strategic Objectives. He reviewed some logistic issues including that there will be 3 days of meetings with some time will be set aside to interact with the local community away from the AC venue. He welcomed ideas for what to prioritize.  Potential topics for the agenda include: challenging how we see the web wrt web apps, AI and browsers; challenges for the web like digital wallets, identity, and provenance; whether the structure of W3C is the right one to tackle challenges; and how to promote community building. There will also be CEO, Board, AB and possibly a TAG report. It was confirmed that sufficient time should be dedicated after each Q&A. Ideas for promoting questions and discussion included: allowing submission of questions ahead of time; limiting responses to one minute; and the AB reaching out to encourage new people to go to the mic. The AB supported opportunities for encouraging more AC members to participate. François confirmed that Members can already bring three representatives to meetings. The AB discussed potentially allowing more Member participants, not just AC reps, to attend or even allowing local technical experts. There was a suggestion that the AB could reach out to new members at the Intro event which will be followed by a coffee break. The Buddy and Mentor systems were mentioned. Angel mentioned a DevMeeup and François also noted there will be a celebration of W3C in China. The breakouts structure was reviewed and the importance of time for central discussion and even follow up on on GitHub was noted. Simultaneous language translation was mentioned and the importance of automatic translation was emphasized. 


* **AI and Human Rights Position Statements**
Elena and Hidde presented first about their AI Position statement. They noted it is their observations and recommendations on AI usage overall, and in particularly how it relates to standards development and that the audience is essentially broader community: team, folks developing standards, and the AB. They noted they were careful not to say that people would need to adhere to any parts of the document but rather that it was a reflection of feedback they received from different W3C Groups. The AB discussed the framing of the risks and responsibilities of using AI and it was suggested that a section noting that people should take responsibility for verifying any W3C work they do with AI. The AB acknowledged that position papers from the AB will carry a certain weight. They noted that certain topics such as use of AI for minutes was out of scope for this statement. 

Dan gave an overview of the Human Rights Position Statement. He reviewed some history of Human Rights and W3C standards; noted the mention of W3C in the technical report from the UN OHCHR; and the inclusion of things like the social impact questionnaire from the TAG. The AB discussed what kind of recommendations would be appropriate to add and what the output might be in terms of impact or recommendations to Team. AB members encouraged focus to be on reflecting human rights in W3C's technical standards work. It was agreed the document should reflect the things W3C is doing and that when we better support human rights, our standards will be more useful. The AB discussed what would be needed to operationalize it and who is the audience for recommendations. 

It was clarified again that AB position statements are meant to be understood as the opinions of the AB, not to represent the entire W3C and that while a position statement might later be submitted for AC review on the Statement track, that wasn't necessarily the goal. AB position statements would be published publicly  - though the means (blog post, note, etc) could be determined.


* **Advisory Committee Representatives, Member Associations and Consortium Member Representatives (with BoD and Seth)**
The AB was joined by Board members and Seth to discuss the roles of AC Reps, Member Associations and Consortium Member Reps. Amy presented an overview of the different descriptions of the roles in our various documents and highlighted some AB discussions and questions from a previous meeting. It was noted that the use of Member Associations had been initiated originally to designate member consortia or groups of members in order to preserve the value of "one member, one vote".  AC reps and Consortium Member reps have different roles relating to different aspects of our work. AC reps participate and vote on the technical standards work of W3C while Consortium Member reps vote on areas related to the business, legal and governance areas of W3C. While some Members have assigned different people in the roles, the distinctions are possibly not widely understood in the Membership.

The group agreed that discrepancies between the Process and Bylaws should be fixed. Some felt that the roles should be combined while others felt they should be kept separate. It was clarified that the Board had delegated the responsibility of determining if a new member was a Member Associations. The group discussed the rationales for limits on non-employee participants from Member Associations and noted they often that they are non-profits. The group suggested clarifying whether there are any legal issues relating to keeping or removing the Member Association designation. It was suggested that instead of designating Ambassadors and sending them out; that a person might first demonstrate they can move things forward before they are designated in the role and also that the role might be by topic (eg: AI) vs. by one specific organization. It was noted that in term of incentives, being an Invited Expert is a valuable role though IE's are also required to follow some formal policies. The suggestion was given to gather topic experts to give feedback on their experiences with outside organizations (eg: "here's what's happening on digital identities"). It was agreed that DingWei would refine the proposal and bring it back.
