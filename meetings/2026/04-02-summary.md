
## 2026-04-02 Advisory Board meeting

What follows is a brief summary of the topics discussed in the W3C Advisory Board meeting on 2026-04-02

attending: Daniel Appelquist, Hidde de Vries, Wei Ding, Max Gendler, Tatsuya Igarashi, Ian Jacobs, An Qi (Angel) Li,  Avneesh Singh, Amy van der Hiel, Brent Zundel

* **Info Share**
Amy gave an update on Team including preparation for AC 2026, W3C@30 Japan, AB nominations, and the W3C AC and Consortium Rep poll. Hidde highlighted that the use of LLMs in standards position statement was noted in the W3C blog and that it got thoughtful feedback in the GH repo. 

* **opportunities for OSS and W3C**
Ian Jacobs joined the AB to give a short review around OSS and W3C that will be presented at the AC meeting in China. The AB discussed different types of OSS projects and also the relationship to W3C.  

* **Open Issue Review & Triage**
Brent led review of open issues in AB repos. Issues reviewed and considered resolved and to be closed included: topics for past F2F meetings; topics for past Monthly Member meetings; anchoring of AB meetings to UTC; affiliation information; legislation citing W3C RECs; AB advice on AC 2026 and AC polling. 

* **AOB**
Angel mentioned current geopolitics and the Consortium's goal to build a better web for humanity. The AB discussed whether or how the AB or W3C might approach addressing such issues. There was a lot of care and nuance in the discussions and a focus on the web being designed to bring people together, the work we do, and W3C as global organization. It was suggested that this might be topic within the community and about how the W3C community relates to each other.
