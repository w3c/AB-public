# Advisory Board meeting

18 December 2025

attending: Amy, Angel, Avneesh Singh, Brent, Dan Appelquist, Dingwei, Elena, Hidde, Tess, Igarashi, Ota, Song

regrets: Max, Emma

Full minutes (member-only link): https://www.w3.org/2025/12/18-ab-minutes.html

## Team Update
Team updates included Team time off for the holidays. Angel and Dan reported that the Board suggested to meet before the F2F. DingWei and Angel noted their proposal for the Exploration IG focus on the Incubation priority. Avneesh gave an update on the work of the Impact Framework Initiative TF and asked the AB to review the TF’s draft Impact Statements and Design Considerations. I gave an update about potential opportunities to work more closely with the Linux Foundation.

## Open Issue Review & Triage
The AB reviewed open issues and moved to close several issues and gave action items, two of which were to update pages to remove mention of the Director and hosts.

## Recap of Monthy Member Meeting
The AB discussed the 16 December Monthly Member meeting that we had organized: On those calls, Darius Kazemi (the prospective co-chair) gave updates on the [proposed Social Web WG charter](https://w3c.github.io/charter-drafts/2025/social-wg.html) at all three sessions. (This is a charter currently out for review.) The TAG’s [Societal Impact Questionnaire](https://w3ctag.github.io/societal-impact-questionnaire/) Draft was also outlined at each session – a document that encourages spec developers to think about the unintended societal consequences of their work. Brent Zundel gave an overview of feedback given to AB on the Process refactoring work and the feedback we had received last month’s TPAC meeting in Japan.

## AB Representative on the AC 2026 program committee
We discussed potential AB representatives on the AC 2026 program committee and suggested both Igarashi and Hidde to be put forward. This will be the program committee organizing the agenda for the forthcoming AC meeting in Hangzhou, China on the week of the 20th of April.

## AB F2F Meeting in London
We confirmed the next AB face-to-face will be held in London on the 4th, 5th and 6th of February, to be hosted by Apple.

## First Draft of AB position paper on AI
Hidde and Elena presented about a first Draft of AB position paper on AI usage in W3C. Hidde noted that the work benefited from them having different perspectives; from the more skeptical to the more optimistic. They asked for AB feedback by the end of January on both format and contents. This paper will have to do with when and how it’s appropriate or not appropriate to use LLMs in the process of doing work in W3C.

## Next meetings
Dan noted that the next meeting would be 8 January, the following meeting will be 22 January and then at the F2F the group will discuss call schedules.
